Tropical Weather Discussion for the eastern Pacific Ocean from

...SPECIAL FEATURES...

Hurricane Fausto is centered near 17.9N 125.3W at 23/1500 UTC,
moving west at 11 kt. Estimated minimum central pressure is 982
mb. Maximum sustained wind speed is 70 kt with gusts to 85 kt. 
Maximum sustained wind speed is 70 kt with gusts to 80 kt. Peak 
seas are near 30 ft. Numerous moderate isolated strong convection
is occurring within 90 nm of center. Fausto is moving toward the
west, and this general motion is expected to continue over the 
next few days. Some small intensity fluctuations are possible 
today, and a slow weakening trend is forecast over the weekend. 
Swells generated by Fausto are affecting portions of the west 
coast of the Baja California Peninsula and the coast of southern 
California. These swells are likely to cause life-threatening 
surf and rip current conditions. Please consult products from 
your local weather office.

Please read the latest HIGH SEAS FORECAST issued by the National
Hurricane Center at website - 
https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/text/MIAHSFEP2.shtml and the latest 
Fausto NHC Forecast/Advisory and Public Advisory at 
www.hurricanes.gov for more details.

Eastern and Central East Pacific (EP98): A tropical wave is along
95.5W. A 1007 mb low is analyzed near 08N95.5W. This system is 
producing a large area of showers and thunderstorms. Environmental
conditions appear conducive for gradual development of this system,
and a tropical depression is likely to form late this week or 
over the weekend, while it moves generally west-northwestward at 
10 to 15 mph, remaining well offshore of Mexico. The latest 
Tropical Weather Outlook gives this system a medium chance of 
tropical cyclone formation through 48 hours, and a high chance 
through 7 days. Please, refer to the latest NHC Tropical Weather 
Outlook at www.hurricanes.gov

...OFFSHORE WATERS WITHIN 250 NM OF MEXICO...

Please refer to the Special Features section for details on Hurricane
Fausto, centered about 915 nm WSW of the southern tip of Baja 
California. Fausto continues to move westward and away from the 
Revillagigedo Islands, allowing high pressure to build in across 
the offshore waters of Baja California and to the Revillagigedo
Islands. This pattern is producing gentle NW to N winds and seas
of 6 to 7 ft in W swell across those area waters. 

A ridge continues to build between Hurricane Fausto and the Baja
California Peninsula. Latest scatterometer data indicated fresh 
to locally strong NW winds north of Punta Eugenia, and moderate 
to locally fresh NW to N winds between Punta Eugenia and Cabo San
Lucas. S to SW swell generating by Fausto is spreading across 
the area, and mixing with the building northerly wind waves to 
produce seas 6 to 8 ft across the Baja waters, highest N of Punta
Eugenia. Gentle to moderate NW to W winds and moderate seas 
prevail across the remainder of the Mexican offshore waters, 
except for fresh N gap winds across the Tehuantepec region and 
downwind to about 15N. 

For the forecast, as Hurricane Fausto continues to move westward
and away from the offshore waters, high pressure will build 
across the Baja California offshore waters through tonight. The 
pressure gradient between the ridge and a meandering trough over 
the Gulf of California will support fresh to strong NW winds and 
building seas N of Punta Eugenia through Thu night into Fri 
morning. Swells generated by Fausto will persist through today 
before fading. These swells are likely to cause life- threatening
surf and rip current conditions. Please consult products from 
your local weather office. In the Gulf of California, mainly 
gentle to moderate southerly winds are expected, increasing to 
fresh speeds Fri night through Sat night. Fresh northerly winds 
are forecast in the Tehuantepec region during the overnight and 
early morning hours through the week, with locally rough seas. 
Please refer to the Special feature section for details on the 
next tropical cyclone forecast to develop well offshore of Mexico
over the upcoming weekend.

...OFFSHORE WATERS WITHIN 250 NM OF CENTRAL AMERICA, COLOMBIA, 
AND WITHIN 750 NM OF ECUADOR...

Latest satellite scatterometer wind data show fresh to strong N
to E winds continuing across the Papagayo region and extending 
downwind to near 91W, and northward to northwestern Nicaragua. 
Seas are 5 to 8 ft with these winds based on altimeter data. This
is occurring behind a tropical wave with axis along 95.5W. The 
monsoon trough remains active across the offshore waters. 
Elsewhere, winds are moderate or weaker with seas 4 to 5 ft in S 
to SW swell.

For the forecast, fresh to strong NE to E winds and moderate to 
rough seas will persist across the Papagayo region through today,
then mainly moderate to fresh winds are expected through Sat 
before increasing to fresh to strong again Sat night and Sun 
night. Gentle to moderate N winds are forecast in the Gulf of 
Panama with slight to moderate seas. Moderate or weaker winds and
moderate seas in SW swell will prevail elsewhere. Seas, associated
with a broad area of developing low pressure currently located 
along the monsoon trough near 08N95.5W, may impact the offshore 
waters of Guatemala and El Salvador later today through Fri, 
building seas to 6 to 8 ft. Strong cross-equatorial swell will 
begin to reach the Galapagos Islands on Fri and build through the
weekend, reaching around 8 ft there by Mon morning.
