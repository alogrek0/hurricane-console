Tropical Weather Discussion

2205 UTC Wed Jul 22 2026

Tropical Weather Discussion for the eastern Pacific Ocean from
03.4S to 30N, east of 120W including the Gulf of California, and
from the Equator to 30N, between 120W and 140W. The following
information is based on satellite imagery, weather observations,
radar, and meteorological analysis.

Based on 1800 UTC surface analysis and satellite imagery through
2100 UTC.
 
...SPECIAL FEATURES...

Hurricane Fausto is centered near 17.2N 122.0W at 22/2100 UTC,
moving west at 10 kt. Estimated minimum central pressure is 983
mb. Maximum sustained wind speed is 70 kt with gusts to 85 kt. 
Peak seas are near 30 ft. Numerous moderate to scattered strong 
convection is occurring within 75 nm of center. Numerous moderate
convection is noted elsewhere from 13N to 17N between 118W and 
122W. Fausto continues west over open waters. A continued
west-northwestward to westward motion, along with an increase in
forward speed, is expected over the next several days. Some gradual 
strengthening is forecast during the next day or so, followed by
slow weakening on Friday and into the weekend. Swells generated by 
Fausto are affecting portions of the west coast of the Baja 
California Peninsula and are spreading northward toward the coast
of southern California. These swells are likely to cause life-
threatening surf and rip current conditions. Please consult products
from your local weather office.

Please read the latest HIGH SEAS FORECAST issued by the National
Hurricane Center at website - 
https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/text/MIAHSFEP2.shtml and the latest 
Fausto NHC Forecast/Advisory and Public Advisory at 
www.hurricanes.gov for more details.

Eastern and Central East Pacific: A tropical wave with axis along
92W continues to produce a large area of disorganized showers 
and thunderstorms. Gradual development of this system is expected,
and a tropical depression is likely to form over the weekend while
it moves generally west-northwestward at 10 to 15 mph, remaining
well offshore of Mexico. The latest Tropical Weather Outlook gives 
this system a medium chance of tropical cyclone formation through
48 hours, and a high chance through 7 days. Please, refer to the
latest NHC Tropical Weather Outlook at www.hurricanes.gov

...TROPICAL WAVES...

A tropical wave is along 92W N of 05N extending into western 
Guatemala, moving westward near 10 kt. A 1008 mb low pressure is
analyzed along the wave axis near 08N92W. Scattered moderate to 
isolated strong convection is seen from 04N to 12N between 87W 
and 95W. Please, see the Special Features section for more 
information related to this wave.

...INTERTROPICAL CONVERGENCE ZONE/MONSOON TROUGH...

The monsoon trough extends from 1006 mb low pressure located over
northern Colombia near 10N75W to 1008 mb low pressure near 08N92W
to 06N105W, then resumes SW of Fausto from 13N128W to beyond 09N140W.
Scattered moderate to isolated strong convection is noted from 
04N to 10N E of 107W to the coast of Colombia. 

...OFFSHORE WATERS WITHIN 250 NM OF MEXICO...

Please refer to the Special Features section for details on Hurricane
Fausto, centered about 760 nm WSW of the southern tip of Baja 
California. Fausto continues to move westward and away from the 
Revillagigedo Islands, leaving gentle to moderate N to NE and 
seas of 6 to 8 ft across those area waters. A recent altimeter 
pass shows seas of 10 to 13 ft within about 145 nm west of Clarion
Island. 

A ridge continues to build between Hurricane Fausto and the Baja
California Peninsula. Recent scatterometer data indicate fresh 
to locally strong NW winds north of Punta Eugenia, and moderate 
to locally fresh NW to N winds between Punta Eugenia and Cabo 
San Lucas. Seas generating by Fausto are spreading across the 
area, with seas up to 8 ft reaching the W coast of Baja California
based on a pair of altimeter passes. Gentle to moderate NW to W 
winds and moderate seas prevail across the remainder of the 
Mexican offshore waters, except for fresh to strong N gap winds 
across the Tehuantepec region and downwind to about 14N. 

For the forecast, as Hurricane Fausto continues to move westward
and away from the offshore waters, high pressure will continue to
build across the Baja California offshore waters tonight through
Thu night. The pressure gradient between the ridge and a meandering
trough over the Gulf of California will support fresh to strong 
NW winds and building seas N of Punta Eugenia through Thu night
into Fri morning. Swells generated by Fausto will persist through
Thu before fading. These swells are likely to cause life- 
threatening surf and rip current conditions. Please consult 
products from your local weather office. In the Gulf of California,
mainly gentle to moderate southerly winds are expected, increasing
to fresh speeds Fri night through Sat night. Fresh northerly winds
are forecast in the Tehuantepec region during the overnight and 
early morning hours through the week, with locally rough seas. 
Please refer to the Special feature section for details on the 
next tropical cyclone forecast to develop well offshore of Mexico
over the upcoming weekend.

...OFFSHORE WATERS WITHIN 250 NM OF CENTRAL AMERICA, COLOMBIA, 
AND WITHIN 750 NM OF ECUADOR...

Latest satellite scatterometer wind data show fresh to strong N
to E winds continuing across the Papagayo region and downwind to
about to 91W. Seas are 5 to 7 ft with these winds based on
altimeter data. This is occurring behind a tropical wave with 
axis along 92W. The monsoon trough remains active across the 
offshore waters N of 04N. Elsewhere, winds are moderate or weaker
with seas 4 to 5 ft in S to SW swell.

For the forecast, fresh to strong NE to E winds and moderate to 
rough seas will persist across the Papagayo region through Thu,
then mainly moderate to fresh winds are expected. Gentle to 
moderate N winds are forecast in the Gulf of Panama with slight 
to moderate seas. Moderate or weaker winds and moderate seas in 
SW swell will prevail elsewhere. Seas, associated with a broad 
area of low pressure currently located along the monsoon trough,
may impact the offshore waters of Guatemala and El Salvador Thu 
through Fri, building seas to 6 to 8 ft.

Special feature section for details on the next tropical cyclone
