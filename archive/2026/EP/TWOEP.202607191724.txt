Tropical Weather Outlook
NWS National Hurricane Center Miami FL
1100 AM PDT Sun Jul 19 2026

For the eastern and central North Pacific east of 180 longitude:

Active Systems:
The National Hurricane Center is issuing advisories on Tropical 
Storm Elida, located well west of the southern tip of the Baja 
California Peninsula, and on Tropical Depression Six-E, located 
several hundred miles southwest of southwestern Mexico.

Eastern and Central East Pacific:
A broad area of low pressure is forecast to develop well south of 
Mexico during the latter part of the week. Environmental 
conditions appear conducive for development of the system 
thereafter, and a tropical depression could form while it moves 
westward to west-northwestward across the eastern and central 
portions of the eastern Pacific.
* Formation chance through 48 hours...low...near 0 percent. 
* Formation chance through 7 days...medium...40 percent.

South-Southwest of Hawaii:
Disorganized showers and thunderstorms located several hundred miles 
south-southwest of the Hawaiian Islands are associated with 
a trough of low pressure.  Environmental conditions appear 
conducive for some gradual development of this system as it moves 
westward and away from the Hawaiian islands during the next 
several days.   
* Formation chance through 48 hours...low...near 0 percent. 
* Formation chance through 7 days...low...20 percent.
