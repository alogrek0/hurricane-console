Tropical Weather Outlook
NWS National Hurricane Center Miami FL
1100 PM PDT Wed Jul 22 2026

For the eastern and central North Pacific east of 180 longitude:

Active Systems:
The National Hurricane Center is issuing advisories on Hurricane 
Fausto, located well west-southwest of the southern tip of the Baja 
California Peninsula.

Eastern and Central East Pacific:
A trough of low pressure located several hundred miles south of the 
coast of southern Mexico continues to produce a large area of 
showers and thunderstorms. Environmental conditions are conducive 
for gradual development of this system, and a tropical depression is 
likely to form late this week into the weekend, while it moves 
generally west-northwestward at 10 to 15 mph, remaining well 
offshore of Mexico.
* Formation chance through 48 hours...medium...60 percent. 
* Formation chance through 7 days...high...90 percent.

Well Southwest of Hawaii:
A trough of low pressure located well southwest of the Hawaiian 
Islands is producing disorganized showers and thunderstorms. 
Environmental conditions appear conducive for some gradual 
development of this system, and a tropical depression is likely to 
form over the weekend or early next week while the system moves 
slowly westward to west-northwestward. The system is forecast to 
move into the western Pacific basin late this weekend or early next 
week.
* Formation chance through 48 hours...low...20 percent. 
* Formation chance through 7 days...high...70 percent.
