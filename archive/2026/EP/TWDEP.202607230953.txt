Tropical Weather Discussion for the eastern Pacific Ocean from
03.4S to 30N, east of 120W including the Gulf of California, and
from the Equator to 30N, between 120W and 140W. The following
information is based on satellite imagery, weather observations,
radar, and meteorological analysis.

Based on 0600 UTC surface analysis and satellite imagery through
0900 UTC.
 
...SPECIAL FEATURES...

Hurricane Fausto is centered near 17.8N 124.0W at 0900 UTC, 
moving west-northwest at 10 kt. Estimated minimum central 
pressure is 982 mb. Maximum sustained wind speed is 70 kt with 
gusts to 80 kt. Peak seas are near 30 ft. Numerous moderate 
isolated strong convection is occurring within 120 nm of center. 
Scattered moderate isolated strong convection is noted elsewhere 
from 12.5N to 20.5N between 117W and 125W. Fausto has begin to
ingest some of the drier air to its west, and the convection is
decreasing on the western semicircle, while thunderstorms tops
are warning. Fausto has begun to accelerate to the west- 
northwestward over open waters, and this faster west- 
northwestward to westward motion expected to continue through the
weekend. Fluctuations in strength are expected through the
weekend, however a general weakening trend is forecast Swells 
generated by Fausto are affecting portions of the west coast of 
the Baja California Peninsula and are reaching the coast of 
southern California. These swells are likely to cause life- 
threatening surf and rip current conditions. Please consult 
products from your local weather office.

Please read the latest HIGH SEAS FORECAST issued by the National
Hurricane Center at website - 
https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/text/MIAHSFEP2.shtml and the latest 
Fausto NHC Forecast/Advisory and Public Advisory at 
www.hurricanes.gov for more details.

Eastern and Central East Pacific: A tropical wave with axis along
94W continues to move into a large area of disorganized showers 
and thunderstorms occurring about the monsoon trough across the
tropical eastern Pacific, between 90W and 103W. A 1008 mb low
pressure center has formed along the monsoon trough near 08N98W,
and ahead of the tropical wave. Gradual organization of this 
system is expected, and a tropical depression is likely to form 
over the weekend while it moves generally west-northwestward at 
10 to 15 mph, remaining well offshore of southwestern Mexico. 
The latest Tropical Weather Outlook gives this system a medium 
chance of tropical cyclone formation through 48 hours, and a high
chance through 7 days. Please, refer to the latest NHC Tropical 
Weather Outlook at www.hurricanes.gov

...OFFSHORE WATERS WITHIN 250 NM OF MEXICO...

Please refer to the Special Features section for details on Hurricane
Fausto, centered about 850 nm WSW of the southern tip of Baja 
California. Fausto continues to move westward and away from the 
Revillagigedo Islands, allowing high pressure to build in across
the offshore waters of Baja California and to the Revillagigedos.
This pattern is producing leaving gentle NW to N winds and seas 
of 6 to 7 ft in W swell across those area waters. Associated
showers and thunderstorms have exited the area to the W.

A ridge continues to build between Hurricane Fausto and the Baja
California Peninsula. Overnight satellite scatterometer data 
indicated fresh to locally strong NW winds north of Punta 
Eugenia, and moderate to locally fresh NW to N winds between 
Punta Eugenia and Cabo San Lucas. S to SW swell generating by 
Fausto is spreading across the area, and mixing with the building
northerly wind waves to produce seas 6 to 8 ft across the Baja 
waters, highest N of Punta Eugenia. Gentle to moderate NW to W 
winds and moderate seas prevail across the remainder of the 
Mexican offshore waters, except for fresh N gap winds across the
Tehuantepec region and downwind to about 15N. Nocturnal
thunderstorms developed along the coast of Sinaloa and are
shifting north and northwestward across the southern Gulf.

For the forecast, as Hurricane Fausto continues to move westward
and away from the offshore waters, high pressure will build 
across the Baja California offshore waters through Thu night. 
The pressure gradient between the ridge and a meandering trough 
over the Gulf of California will support fresh to strong NW winds
and building seas N of Punta Eugenia through Thu night into Fri 
morning. Swells generated by Fausto will persist through Thu 
before fading. These swells are likely to cause life- threatening
surf and rip current conditions. Please consult products from 
your local weather office. In the Gulf of California, mainly 
gentle to moderate southerly winds are expected, increasing to 
fresh speeds Fri night through Sat night. Fresh northerly winds 
are forecast in the Tehuantepec region during the overnight and 
early morning hours through the week, with locally rough seas. 
Please refer to the Special feature section for details on the 
next tropical cyclone forecast to develop well offshore of Mexico
over the upcoming weekend.

...OFFSHORE WATERS WITHIN 250 NM OF CENTRAL AMERICA, COLOMBIA, 
AND WITHIN 750 NM OF ECUADOR...

Overnight satellite scatterometer wind data show fresh to strong
E winds continuing across the Papagayo region and extending 
downwind to near 90W, and northward to northwestern Nicaragua. 
Seas are 5 to 8 ft with these winds based on altimeter data. 
This is occurring behind a tropical wave with axis along 94W. 
The monsoon trough remains active across the offshore waters, 
producing scattered to locally numerous convection N of 05N to
11N, from Panama and Costa Rica westward. Elsewhere, winds are 
moderate or weaker with seas 4 to 5 ft in S to SW swell.

For the forecast, fresh to strong NE to E winds and moderate to 
rough seas will persist across the Papagayo region through Thu,
then mainly moderate to fresh winds are expected through Sat
before increasing to fresh to strong again Sat night and Sun
night. Gentle to moderate N winds are forecast in the Gulf of 
Panama with slight to moderate seas. Moderate or weaker winds and
moderate seas in SW swell will prevail elsewhere. Seas, 
associated with a broad area of developing low pressure 
currently located along the monsoon trough, may impact the 
offshore waters of Guatemala and El Salvador Thu through Fri, 
building seas to 6 to 8 ft. Strong cross-equatorial swell will
begin to reach the Galapagos Islands on Fri and build through the
weekend, reaching around 8 ft there by Mon morning.
