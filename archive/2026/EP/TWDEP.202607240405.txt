...SPECIAL FEATURES...

Hurricane Fausto is centered near 18.3N 128.4W, or about 1075 nm 
west of the southern tip of Baja California at 24/0300 UTC, 
moving west at 13 kt. Estimated minimum central pressure is 976 
mb. Maximum sustained wind speed is 80 kt with gusts to 100 kt. 
Peak seas are near 32 ft. Satellite imagery shows that Fausto has
appears slightly stronger than the past few hours. Numerous 
moderate to isolated strong convection is within 150 nm of the 
center over in the E semicircle, within 180 nm of the center in 
the SW quadrant and within 120 nm of the center in the NW 
quadrant. Fausto is forecast to maintain its present motion 
over the next few days. Little change in intensity is forecast 
through tonight, followed by steady weakening through the 
weekend. Swells generated by Fausto are affecting the west coast
of the Baja California Peninsula and the coast of southern 
California. These swells are likely to cause life-threatening 
surf and rip current conditions. Please consult products from 
your local weather office.

Please read the latest HIGH SEAS FORECAST issued by the National
Hurricane Center at website - 
https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/text/MIAHSFEP2.shtml and the latest 
Fausto NHC Forecast/Advisory and Public Advisory at 
www.hurricanes.gov for more details.

Storm Warning: Low pressure, Invest EP98 near 09N97W 1007 mb, 
is gradually becoming better organized. Winds associated with 
this low pressure are expected to reach gale-force in about 24
hours or sooner, and storm-force of 45 to 55 kt afterward.
Presently, winds are 20 to 30 kt with the low, but are expected 
to build rather quickly Fri through Sat night as the winds reach
storm-force. Seas are expected to reach at peak of around 18 ft 
by late Sat. Environmental conditions appear conducive for 
additional development of this system, and a tropical depression 
or storm is likely to form within the next couple of days, while 
the system moves generally west-northwestward and remains well 
offshore of Mexico. The low pressure has a high chance of 
developing into a tropical depression of storm during the next 
couple of days.

...INTERTROPICAL CONVERGENCE ZONE/MONSOON TROUGH...

The monsoon trough extends from low pressure over northwest
Colombia to 08N78W to 10N90W to low pressure, Invest EP98,
near 09N97W to 08N107W and to 09N116W. It resumes SW of 
Fausto near 11N139W to beyond 140W. Scattered moderate to
isolated strong convection is within 90 nm south of the
trough between 78W-85W. 

...OFFSHORE WATERS WITHIN 250 NM OF MEXICO...

Please refer to the Special Features section for details on 
Hurricane Fausto, centered about 1075 nm W of the southern tip 
of Baja California. Fausto continues to move westward and away 
from the Revillagigedo Islands, allowing a ridge to build in 
across the offshore waters of Baja California and to the 
Revillagigedo Islands. This pattern supports fresh to strong NW 
winds north of Punta Eugenia, and mainly gentle to moderate NW to
N winds between Punta Eugenia and Cabo San Lucas. Seas 
generating by Fausto are still spreading across the area, and 
mixing with the building northerly wind waves to produce seas 6 
to 8 ft across the Baja waters, highest N of Punta Eugenia. 
Gentle to moderate NW to W winds and moderate seas prevail across
the remainder of the Mexican offshore waters.

For the forecast, as Hurricane Fausto continues to move westward
high pressure will continue to build across the Baja California 
offshore waters through tonight. The pressure gradient between 
high pressure that builds in over the region in the wake of 
Fausto and a trough over the Gulf of California will induce fresh
to strong winds and rough seas N of Punta Eugenia through Fri 
morning. Fresh to strong northerly winds are forecast during the 
overnight hours and into the mornings through the upcoming 
weekend. Looking ahead, an area of low pressure located several 
hundred nautical miles south of the coast of southern Mexico is 
showing some signs of organization. Environmental conditions 
appear conducive for additional development of this system, and a
tropical depression or storm is likely to form within the next 
couple of days, while the system moves generally west- 
northwestward remaining well offshore of Mexico.

...OFFSHORE WATERS WITHIN 250 NM OF CENTRAL AMERICA, COLOMBIA, 
AND WITHIN 750 NM OF ECUADOR...

Fresh to strong N to E winds continue across the Papagayo region
and extending downwind to near 91W, and northward to 
northwestern Nicaragua. Seas are 5 to 8 ft with these winds based
on altimeter data. This is occurring behind a tropical wave with
axis along 96W. The monsoon trough remains active across the 
offshore waters. Elsewhere, winds are moderate or weaker with 
seas 4 to 5 ft in S to SW swell.

For the forecast, fresh to strong NE to E winds will pulse at 
night and into the morning across the Papagayo region through 
Sat. Moderate or weaker winds along with moderate seas in SW 
swell will remain elsewhere. Seas associated with a developing 
low pressure system near 09N97W may impact the offshore waters of
Guatemala through Fri, building seas to around 9 ft. Cross- 
equatorial southerly swell will begin to reach the Galapagos 
Islands on Fri, and build through the weekend and into Mon before
is begins to decay on Tue.
